Public Speaking for Busy People

  • What you have to say is important.
  • If you're here today, whether you're a member or you're just considering becoming a member, it's because you know you have something to say.
  • For some of you there's somthing standing in the way
  • For some people it's nerves, and I can tell you than in my 13 years in Toastmasters I have seen dozens of people say goodbye to the quiver that was in their voice when they started.
  • But today I want to talk about another obstacle that seems to come up just as often: time.
  • Everyone's so busy. Too busy to work on Toastmasters, they say.
  • So today I want to share some tips that I use to get my speeches done when I'm really busy.
  • And I'm not even going to use Chat-GPT!

Just write something

https://twitter.com/ESYudkowsky/status/1648880205967114241

  • It's true that you'll get out of Toastmasters what you put into it. If you put in more time, you'll get more experience.
  • But if you can't put in as much time as you'd like, don't let that paralyze you.
  • You will always learn more from doing something rather than nothing.
  • "Lose your first 50 games as quickly as possible."
  • The best way to improve at speaking is to give your first 50 bad speeches as fast as possible.

Write your speech backwards

  • Start with a call to action -- what is one thing you want your audience to do or know?
  • Write down three supporting points
  • You have your speech outline.
  • worst case: you could give your speech now
  • It's better to prepare more details, but you don't have to

Use notes if you have to

Even if you can't remember your 3 main points, you can put them on a notecard (or your screen). Even if you literally read your speech, you will still get practice:
  • Organizing your thoughts
  • Using your voice
  • Using gestures
Even the president uses a teleprompter sometimes.
