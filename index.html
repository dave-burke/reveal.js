Toastmasters for Busy People

  • If you're here today it's because you know you have something to say.
  • Well you're in the right place because we'd all like to help you say it.
  • But sometimes things get in the way.
  • Today I want to talk about an obstacle I hear about a lot: time.
  • Everyone's so busy. Too busy to work on Toastmasters, they say.
  • So today I want to share some tips that I use to get my speeches done when I'm really busy.

Just write something

https://twitter.com/ESYudkowsky/status/1648880205967114241

  • I'm not telling you not to do your best
  • You get out what you put in to TM
  • if you can't put in as much time as you'd like, don't let that paralyze you.
  • You will always learn more from doing something rather than nothing.

Use notes if you have to

Yes, no notes is a skill worth having.

But if you don't have time to work on that skill, you will still get practice:

  • Organizing your thoughts
  • Using your voice for emphasis
  • Using gestures

If the President can use a teleprompter, you can use notes.

You will never not be busy

  • It's so easy to keep telling yourself what you'll accomplish "when things settle down"
  • "After I graduate" "After we settle in to the new house" "After the baby sleeps through the night" "When I'm not driving my kids to activities all the time" "When work isn't so hectic" "When I retire"
  • If you're going to be busy anyway, then get busy improving your ability to communicate effectively.
  • I think you'll find that it's easier to get other things done when you feel more at ease talking to co-workers, friends, or even your kids.
  • Don't worry about being perfect
  • Use notes if it helps you
  • Don't wait until you stop feeling busy

What's your next step?

  • Wherever you're at:

Take the next step in your Toastmasters journey. No matter how big or how small it has to be.
